\n Mixed Doubles (or “XD”) has a long history in tennis with the first grand slam mixed match being played at\n the\n 1887 US Open Championships. It was also included as an event in the Olympics in 1900, 1912, 1920, and 1924\n but\n was excluded from the Olympics held between 1925 and 2011. However, in 2012, XD was included again as an\n event\n at the London Olympics and was also included as an event at the 2016 Rio de Janeiro Olympics and the 2020\n Tokyo\n Olympics. It is also now included as an event at each of the four grand slam tournaments (Australian Open,\n French Open, Wimbledon, and US Open).\n

\n Tennis Mixed\n Doubles Rules\n
\n In general, tennis mixed doubles rules are the same as for regular doubles. Serving rules are the same, as each\n player (both male or female) serves until a game is won or lost. Each player will serve to both players in the\n opposing team, and vice versa. Scoring rules are generally the same, with a 10-point tiebreaker as the deciding set.\n\n
